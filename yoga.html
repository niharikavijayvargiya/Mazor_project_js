Surya Namaskar, or Sun Salutation, is a sequence of twelve yoga postures performed in a fluid motion to salute the sun and energize the body. It involves a combination of forward bends, backward bends, and strengthening poses, promoting flexibility, strength, and mental clarity. Practicing Surya Namaskar regularly can enhance overall physical and mental well-being.
Surya Namaskar
Padmasana or Lotus Pose derives its name from the Sanskrit word for lotus. To truly enjoy the benefits of this asana, sit in a cross-legged position with an erect spine and both feet on the opposite thighs. Padmasana helps open up your hips, stretches your ankles and heels. It calms your mind, helps with brain functioning, and reduces muscle tension.
Paschimottanasana
It is known as the corrector of the curvature and makes the spine flexible. The curve structure of the asana massages the deep back muscles, spine and nerves. It can be a great asana for people dealing with arthritis of the lower back and lower back pains. It relieves menstrual problems by stretching the uterus and ovaries. It releases stress through invigorating adrenal glands and kidneys.
Bhujangasana

Yoga holds profound significance in fostering holistic well-being, encompassing the body, mind, and spirit. Beyond its physical benefits, which include increased flexibility, strength, and balance, yoga serves as a potent tool for mental and emotional wellness. Through its practices of deep breathing, meditation, and mindfulness, yoga cultivates inner peace, reduces stress, and enhances mental clarity. Moreover, yoga encourages self-awareness and introspection, fostering a deeper connection with oneself and promoting personal growth. Its philosophies promote values such as compassion, gratitude, and self-discipline, nurturing a harmonious relationship with oneself and others. As a versatile practice accessible to people of all ages and fitness levels, yoga transcends cultural boundaries, offering a universal path towards health, happiness, and spiritual fulfillment.
